**FAQ:**

1. **How many creative ad sets can you launch at a time/how many ads can i test**
	- If you're only spending few hundred a day, I recommend keeping it to 5 ad sets (including champions ad set, if it has ads inside) at a time so budget can be distributed efficiently. Focus more on intent of the ads instead of throwing things at the wall.
		- You can launch all 5 ad sets in the same day if you wish
		- **If you want the exact calculation on how to figure out the max number of creatives to test per week:**
		Example #1 - Budget $100/day
		Total Budget (example $100/day)
		Target CPA (Example $20)
		Total Ad Sets = Budget/TCPA
		$100/$20 = 5
		So the maximum amount of concepts is 5, considering 100% of your budget is testing.
		- **If you're spending a few thousand a day you can definitely have more ad sets due to budget and CPA**
2. **Do we set min/max spend limits for this campaign method?**
	- No, we tested it and it didn't help.
3. **How long do you test creatives for?**
	- We recommend testing creatives for a minimum of 3 days. However, we now leave them testing 7 days. If they don't do well after 7 days (not much spending or missing KPI), we will then turn them off. If you want to test ads longer, you can. But we usually don't recommend leaving ads on for longer than 14 days.
4. **How do you know if a creative ad set is a winner (Covered in "When To Leave a 3:2:2 Running" video below)?**
	- If it gets majority of spend at KPI like shown in the video. Besides that if you're still not sure, the question you should ask is "Did my overall campaign performance improve?". If your overall campaign ROAS didn't improve, it's not a winner.
5. **When do you turn off creatives/ad sets (Covered in "When To Turn Off A 3:2:2" video below)?**
	- With this method we are only turning off at the ad set level not ad level.
		- If the ad set is still not getting much spend after 7 days, and ROAS is not good we will turn off. If ROAS is good you may leave it running if you have no other creatives in queue.
		- If the ad set took majority of spend within the first 3 days, performance is not good and overall campaign performance went down, we will turn off. But we will do learnings on the ad because something about it got a lot of engagement, so make it more conversion driven. Try to give it 2-3 days to test, it may not convert that well 1st day but 2nd or 3rd day pick up.
		- No we don't turn off ads that were doing well but suddenly tanked, zoom out look at averages instead
6. **What's the minimum budget to launch this?**
	- Minimum I would start this campaign at is $100/day
7. **What if I don't have any past winners to put in the champions ad set or it's a new brand**
	- You can set up the champions ad set still just don't launch any ads inside there until you have a proven winner you can drop inside.
8. **Can you mix images and videos in this same campaign?**
	- Yes, that is fine but keep them separate ad sets. 1 concept should be 1 ad set, so 1 image concept with 3 variations.

Extra FAQ for Product Testing

**1) Is there a special campaign you use for product testing?**

No, it's still the same broad 1 CBO campaign as shown in the video above

**2) How long do you test a product for before moving on?**

The answer to this depends on your skill level. If you're a complete beginner, it's much better to test a product for 3 days, and if there's no traction (your goal is to get something to breakeven) then you can move on to the next product.

This way, you take more "Medium intent" swings rather than going high intent and low volume. At the beginning stages, you need volume, because you don't know what good and bad looks like yet. You will build up your skills faster this way.

If you try to make everything perfect before launching, it takes too much time, and the extra time you spent could be trash too, since you don't know what good and bad looks like.

If you're more experienced and trying to start a new E-Commerce brand now, you can test it for 1-2 weeks while continually adding new ads to test. Since we know you can make any product work, it might just take 1 winning ad in that period to turn things around.

**3) Should I touch the ads or kill products early based on the metrics?**  
  
People will tell you they kill based on CTR, CPC, cost per add to cart, etc

I don't really care about any of that. All I care about is spend and ROAS. Because if you have an ad that gets only $0.01 spend but has a high CTR, it's still a bad ad.

The goal is to have your product break even during the initial test. If it's at least breakeven,n it means you can invest in improving things to make it profitable and scale now.

Continue testing for the 3 days and see if the product has traction or not; don't need to turn off any ads until then.

How to plan to get to $1,000+ daily

**The core idea**

You don't have a knowledge problem. You have a focus problem. Most people are productive just on the wrong things. This system helps you focus on what actually moves you forward, structured so every daily action compounds into weekly and monthly progress.

**The 2 buckets** *(assuming you already have a product live)*

- **Ads** — how you bring people in *(this is your biggest lever)*
- **CRO** — how to maximise revenue per session

If you nail your ads, you'll convert even with an average offer and page. The reverse isn't true a great page with bad ads goes nowhere because nobody's coming. So when in doubt, focus on ads.

**The 3 time layers**

- **Month** — the direction. One goal per bucket. Directional, measurable, realistic but a stretch.
- **Week** — the bridge. What needs to be true by Friday for the month to stay on track? 1–2 things per bucket.
- **Day** — the action. Pull 1–2 tasks from the week. Time block them. Plan the night before.

Each layer feeds the one below it. Daily reps → weekly outcomes → monthly direction.

**Example: a real month**

*Where I am: $100/day in sales. Where I want to go: $300/day in 30 days.*

- **Ads** — Launch 3–5 ad batches per week *(goal: find at least 1 winner this month)*
- **CRO** — Launch a "Buy 2, Save 15%" bundle

Notice all are inputs you control. You can't force an ad to win, but you can ship the volume that makes a winner likely. Focus on the input; the outcome follows.

**How to define winners:**  
  
💸 Spend Winners - ads that get 30%-50% or more of spend but do NOT allow you to increase budget

🏆 Breakthroughs – ads that get more than 30-50% or more of campaign spend & allow you to increase daily budget while maintaining your target ROAS.

**Your goal is breakthroughs.** They're harder to find; expect a lower win rate across that. A breakthrough is the key to scaling. A spend winner just keeps you where you are.

**As you scale**

The buckets get more granular as you grow — at $1k/day, "ads" splits into media buying, creative, and scripting; at $10k/day, it splits further. But the framework never changes. Month informs week, week informs day. Only the resolution gets sharper.

🆕 Marksman VS Sniper Method Explained

7:33

**3 - 2 - 2 = Our testing strategy in Meta**

**3 Ad Creatives --> Marksman = 3 reasons why someone should buy (3 Angles) --> 1 way of communicating each angle**

2 primary copies --> Keep it more generic (about the product or offer, not the angle)

2 headlines --> Keep it more generic (about the product or offer, not the angle)

## The Example:

**Product:** Shirt

**Angle 1:** Makes You Attractive

**Ad Creative 1:** Makes your arms look bigger than they are *(get girls who like big arms lol)*  

**Angle 2:** You Are One Of Us

**Ad Creative 1:** For those who love America  

**Angle 1:** Comfort

**Ad Creative 1:** It feels like your wearing a cloud

**3 Ad Creatives --> Sniper = 1 reason why someone should buy (1 Angle) --> 3 different ways of communicating each angle**

2 primary copies --> Make it more specific (about the angle, can also talk about product/offer)

2 headlines --> Make it more specific (about the angle, can also talk about product/offer)

## The Example:

**Product:** Shirt

**Angle 1:** Comfort

**Ad Creative 1:** It feels like your wearing a cloud

**Ad Creative 2:** It's as soft as a babies bottom

I learned a lot from this product even if it failed.

I spent two weeks building a supplement with a new mechanism around a product people already knew, but positioned for bloating. It did not work, and I only got a few ATCs and 2 unprofitable sales.

What I learned is that my copy still needs work. Sometimes my hook was problem-aware, but the bridge became solution-aware, so the message was not fully congruent. I also learned that product selection matters a lot. It is easier to sell products when people are already well educated on the benefit and when the mechanism improves something people already truly want.

I also got better at reading data, understanding awareness levels, and seeing that each sub avatar has its own level of awareness. On top of that, I realized how important research, swiping proven scripts, and breaking down strong copy every day really are as a daily exercise.

So yes, it failed, but it made me better. Now I move to the next product with more skill, better research, and a clearer understanding of what actually matters.

Set your target CPA first. Let’s say real breakeven is around $20–25.

At $50/day, I wouldn’t judge purely by “how many days.” I’d judge by time, spend, and signals.

⭐ IMPORTANT NOTE #2
Prior to setting up any offer ALWAYS use the proﬁt calculator to establish the new targets
for ROAS & CPA so you know whether or not it is successful
Evergreen Example:
If your ROAS is currently a 2x and your break even ROAS is 1.43x (70% Gross proﬁt) and your
spending $100k per month, and you’re generating $200k total.
Your Gross Proﬁt = $40,000 ($200k rev *0.7 gp = $140k gp - $100k spend)
New Offer Example:
But if you launch a 30% offer Keeps you at a 2x ROAS but your breakeven is now 1.82x (55%
Gross proﬁt) and now your spending $150k per month, generating $300k total
Gross Proﬁt = $15,000 ($300k rev *0.55 gp = $165k gp - $150k spend)

## Key Takeaways

- **Creative is the primary lever for scaling.** Solve high CPMs and stalled growth by testing new creative angles and iterating on winners.
- **Prioritize first-order profitability for cash flow.** This enables sustainable scaling, especially for subscription brands, before pursuing longer payback windows.
- **Test ads for a minimum of 7 days.** This allows the algorithm to find its audience and prevents prematurely killing ads that are just finding their stride.
- **Use simple structures for testing.** A single CBO with concept-based ad sets is ideal. Test radical new ideas in separate ABOs to protect the main campaign's performance.

## Topics

### Creative & Testing Strategy

- **Testing Timeline:** A 7-day minimum is recommended for testing ads.
	- **Rationale:** Allows the algorithm sufficient time to find its audience.
		- **Exception:** If cash flow is tight and no buying intent is seen after 3 days, pause the ad.
- **Creative Iteration vs. New Ideas:** The ratio is a sliding scale based on performance.
	- **With a Winner:** Focus on iterating on the winner to maximize its lifespan.
		- **Without a Winner:** Prioritize testing new ideas to find the next breakthrough.
		- **KPI:** Track the "winning ad rate" (winners per ads launched), not just the number of ads.
- **Scaling from $1k/day to $10k/day:** The main lever is creative.
	- **Action:** Convert winning static ad messages into video formats.
		- **Focus:** Keep the setup simple (e.g., direct to PDP) until monthly revenue exceeds six figures.
- **High CPMs:** A symptom of poor creative, not a metric to fix directly.
	- **Solution:** Launch a new, better-performing ad. One participant saw CPMs drop from $150 to $30 after launching a new winner.
- **Organic Content Strategy:**
	- **Principles:** Ad creation principles apply to organic content, but organic allows for more aggressive claims.
		- **Volume:** High volume is especially critical for TikTok organic.
		- **Synergy:** Run top-performing organic content as paid ads. Slideshows are also performing well.

### Campaign & Scaling Tactics

- **Scaling Stalled at $300/day:** The performance drop when scaling from $300 to $500/day indicates the current ads have a spend limit.
	- **Action:** Revert to the profitable budget ($300/day) and test new creative angles until a new winner is found.
- **Ad Set Structure:**
	- **Standard:** Use one CBO with concept-based ad sets (e.g., 3 ads + 3 variations per concept).
		- **Radical Tests:** Use a separate ABO campaign for completely new or "wild" ad concepts.
		- **Rationale:** Protects the main CBO from being derailed by a high-spend, low-converting ad.

### AOV & Profitability

- **AOV Improvement for a Single-SKU Brand:**
	- **Offer:** Test "Buy 2, Get 1 Free" or "Buy 1, Get 1 at a higher price" offers.
		- **Post-Purchase Upsells:**
		- **Email:** Send a 30% off email for a second purchase, with a 12-24 hour urgency timer.
				- **Apps:** Start with Aftersell (affordable) before considering Order Editing ($400/mo).
- **Subscription Brand Profitability:**
	- **Priority:** Optimize for first-order profitability to ensure cash flow.
		- **Shipping:** Test charging for shipping. For an AOV of £62, a £3 charge is unlikely to impact conversion but will improve margins.

### Personal & Business Development

- **Digital Nomad Setup:**
	- **Financial Readiness:** The required profit/savings depend entirely on the chosen jurisdiction's tax and residency rules.
		- **Action:** Consult a tax advisor or use an AI tool (e.g., Claude) for specific cost and tax estimates.
- **Re-engaging After a Slump:**
	- **Action:** Re-learn fundamentals by diligently completing all training modules.
		- **Mindset:** Focus on building genuine skills, not just chasing quick wins.

## Next Steps

- **Diego:** Simplify the testing process by focusing on creative angles with a basic PDP, rather than complex advertorials.
- **ER:** Prioritize first-order profitability and test adding a shipping fee to improve margins.
- **Ralph:** Revert to the profitable $300/day budget and focus on testing new creative angles to find a new winner.

- **Bamba:** Reduce the number of ad sets in the CBO to improve algorithm efficiency.
- **Tanuj:** Test combining AI avatars with authentic UGC in new video ads.

**12-Year-Old Definition:** The number you use to keep track of which test you're on

**Examples:**  
*Batch #1, Batch #29, Batch #133*

**12-Year-Old Concept Definition:** The big idea behind the ads you want to test

**Examples:***  
"Test an iteration on a winning video."*

*"Test superlative angles."*

*"Test UGC format"*

*"Test different avatars."*

**🔗Angle** ⇒The selling strategy - how you're choosing to sell the product

**12-Year-Old Angle Definition:** The main reason you're giving someone to buy  
**Examples:**  
*"Buy It For Life"*

*"Postpartum weight despite trying everything"*

*"Stainless steel / Doctor-designed / Cleanest"*

*Key insight: Every ad needs an angle. Even if your concept is "testing formats," you still need to define what you're selling. If your concept is “Testing X Angle” the concept becomes the angle. But for tracking purposes, I prefer to actually JOT DOWN the angle twice so we can be sure.  
  
*  
**🔀Variations** ⇒ Individual executions of the angle(s)  
**12-Year-Old Variations Definition:** Different ways to communicate your selling reason(s)

**Example:***  
If your angle is "Buy It For Life," variations might be: "One harness, lifetime of adventures," "Buy it once, adventure for life," or "End the replacement cycle"  
  
*

*If testing multiple angles, variations might be different angles like "Stainless steel," "Doctor-designed," "Easiest to clean."  
  
*🖼️ **Format** ⇒ How you deliver the ad

**12-Year-Old Format Definition:** The type of ad you're making

*Example: You could show the same message as a video of a real person talking, a slideshow of pictures, or just a product image.

Why the fact it’s hard is actually good

14:05

- If something were easy, everyone would have it. Difficulty is a **filter** that separates those who want it from those who deserve it.
- The “hardness” of something is what **creates scarcity**. Scarcity = value.

> ***“The fact that most people quit is the reason you shouldn’t.”***
> 
> *\- Someone smarter than me*

---

### Emotional Framing

- Every frustration (No winning ads, low ROAS, losing money) is proof that you’re in the arena. Most aren’t.
- Instead of saying “this is hard,” say: “This is the part that makes it worth it.”  
	That mindset flips pain into proof of progress.
- The very reason most people *don’t* have it, is the same reason it’s *so rewarding* when you finally do.

> ***The fact that it’s hard is what makes it worth it.***
> 
> *\- Mozi*

**...Also, it’s not really that hard lol...**

### 🕰️ Then vs. Now — “Starting a Business in the 1990s vs. 2020s”

**THEN (1990s)**

- Had to rent office space — even if you were solo.
- Needed landlines, fax machines, and physical business cards.
- Building a website? Cost $10K–$50K and took months.
- Marketing? Yellow Pages, newspaper ads, or local radio.
- To reach customers outside your city, you needed a warehouse, shipping partners, and *trust* built through mail-order catalogs.
- To hire? You posted in local classifieds. Couldn’t tap into global talent.
- Raising money meant begging banks or investors for a loan and wearing a suit.
- Scaling beyond your town required *logistics*, not just clicks.

**NOW (2020s+)**

- You can start a brand from your couch with $1,000 and a laptop.
- No-code tools (Shopify, Webflow, Zapier) let you launch a full business in a day.
- You can test ideas with AI, market research, ad copy, product names, instantly.
- You can hire people from the Philippines, Pakistan, or Eastern Europe for a fraction of local cost.
- You can reach 10,000,000 people overnight with one viral video or ad for FREE.
- You can receive instant feedback from comments, analytics, or surveys.
- You can sell globally, automatic translations, global payment processors, fulfillment handled by Amazon or 3PLs.
- You can learn *everything* for free on YouTube, X, or inside communities like Origins for less than $100.
- You can literally talk to an AI team 24/7 to help you brainstorm, design, and execute.

---

> In 1995, if you wanted to start a supplement brand, you’d need to convince a manufacturer to take your call, spend $30K on product runs, $10K on design, and pray you could get your product into local stores.
> 
> In 2025, you can get a Shopify template, prompt Claude for your product description, hire a designer on Fiverr, and run your first ad on Meta all before lunch.

---

### 💡 Data & Historical Examples

- **Amazon** took *three years* to hit $1M/month in revenue.  
	→ Many Shopify stores hit that in less than 6 months today.
- **Nike’s first ad** in the ‘70s cost $10,000 and reached thousands.  
	→ A single UGC video can reach millions organically.
- **Hiring developers** in 1995: $80K+ salary, on-site.  
	→ Hiring developers in 2025: $2K/month, remote from anywhere.
