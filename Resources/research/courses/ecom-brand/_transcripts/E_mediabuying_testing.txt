The 3 Testing Methods
Now, the biggest thing when it comes to “how you test angles” and actually turning the
information and angles that you found from the avatar training into ADS.

Is knowing that no matter what, you are NOW AWARE.
So, if you are struggling to combine things, which hopefully you aren't, the main, main takeaway
is that.
Now again, the point of doing the avatar work that we just did is so that you have Angles that
are actually relatable for your Concepts.
But now the question is, how do we actually go about testing all of these angles that we have in
an organized way?
Well, that's why I want to discuss the 3 types of ways you can “test angles” or, really, “test ads”
in general.
1.​ Marksman Method - Test a lot of things at once to see what sticks, but in a
controlled environment.

​
​
The reason why it's called the Marksman Method is that for some reason I refer to advertising

strategies using “gun analogies” (I’m an Call of duty player)​
​
Most of you are probably aware of the two most common gun analogies, which are
“Shotgun” & “Sniper” → Which are things we're going to get into.
The Marksman Method is basically the middle ground of them both.
With this method, you are NOT as honed in as a sniper, but you're also not as careless as a
shotgunner.
You are the best of both.​
​
Pros Of The Marksman Method: Much more fluid, less “we have to stay within these lines,”
and you really are focused on making the best ads possible.
Mainly, you will learn which Direction you should go in FASTER when you lack significant data.​
​
Direction can be defined as: identifying the specific angle that you should be going
“deeper on” (aka doing more research + iterating on) to create more scalable ads (example
coming below)

🌟

Cons Of The Marksman Method: You aren’t always able to test things the best way possible,
and it can be hard to confidently say “This is why something is working” (although that’s hard
regardless)
Overall, you're not able to go as deep into a specific ad Angle with the Marksman Method to
fully validate and test it. BUT, you can test more at once in order to find a direction that you can
then go deeper on.

2.​ Sniper Method - Test within a strict environment to get closer to significant
learnings as fast as possible.

​
Now, unlike the Marksman method above, where you are essentially testing multiple angles at
once,
The Sniper Method is ALL about testing different executions/styles of a specific angle.
Essentially, once you find an angle that you know is working, you then leverage the sniper
method to go all in on that angle in order to create the best ads possible.
Pros: You will have a clearer understanding of what works, be more organized, learn what
“works” faster, and have more confidence that you have validated specific angles.​
​
Sniper Method REALLY crushes once you gain a direction from the Marksman Method
so you can get the most out of angle.​

🌟

Cons: Much less fluid, restricts creativity, can hold you back from making “the best ads”, most
people are unable to stay this focused on 1 specific direction, and can put you into an iteration
overload.
Overall, you're able to go MUCH deeper on a specific ad concept to fully validate and test it. But
you can lack the ability to find breakthroughs on other angles because of how specific you are
being.​

​
If it wasn’t obvious above, here’s how we leverage these 2 testing strategies at the agency: ​
​
→ The Marksman Method is used 1st to find the direction of what angles can be winners
→ The Sniper Method is used 2nd to go deeper & find winners once a direction has been found
→ The Marksman Method is then used again when performance plateaus on an angle.
When you have ONE Angle = you're taking more of a “Sniper Approach” (same Angle, different
ways to say it/show it)
When you have MULTIPLE Angles = you're taking more of a “Marksman Approach” (Different
Angles AND usually only 1 way to say it)​
​

The #1 Main Takeaway: ​
​
Regardless of the approach, just make the BEST ADS you
can. ​
​
Then, when you’re processing feedback loops, be AWARE of
the avatars you're targeting.​
Nowadays, it’s nearly impossible to do perfect “single-variable” testing as a result of Meta’s
newest updates. The swings are “too small” and not significant enough at times for Meta to
care.
If the Marksman Method & Sniper Method aren’t making sense, here is another way to put it.
We currently test a lot of new angles early on for new ads that we make.
AND THEN
When we find angles that pick up traction (aka get spend) we go more into “single angle”
testing, which also means making iterations & variations (we will define these as well)
So,
Step 1: Test multiple angles initially to find something that “gets sales” or “wins”
Step 2: Make hypotheses on why something is winning

Step 3: Test those hypotheses to narrow down why something is winning to optimize it more.​
​
The first hardest part is step one, and that's what we're going to get into in the next video.
But once you do that, steps 2 and 3 become the easy part because of the knowledge that you
have as a result of going through this training.
And that's going to be your “super power”
Most people NEVER know why something is winning.
They take broad learnings like:
“Videos ads work,” or “This has a good hook rate,” or “We should reuse this clip”, or “We should
test a UGC version.”
But when you process learnings, you're going to be able to identify who the sub-avatar is, what
the angle is, and then be able to go and do more research on that so that you can write new ads
that are focused on the customer rather than the structural components.
The faster you figure out why something IS NOT winning, the FASTER you can scale.
The faster you figure out why something IS winning, the FASTER you can scale.

#3 - The Shotgun Method - Test a bunch of random things at once to see what sticks

​

The shotgun method is when you just launch a bunch of ads and hope something hits.
The right way to do this is through TikTok Shop content or our Insense product seeding/UGC
strategy.
You get a bunch of content coming in from a bunch of different creators, and you don't really
have time to go through and strategize every single one. So, you just launch all of the content in
your ad account & hope that one of them hits.
And if they do, then you can process learnings to identify the angle, and use the Marksman
Method or Sniper Method to refine it to create more winning ads
This is actually something that we did recently to scale a brand to 40K per day in spend.
Unfortunately, I cannot share that exact ad, but I promise you this strategy crushes.

The #1 Mistake Brands Make:
The WRONG way to do the shotgun strategy is WITH your creative strategy team.
DO NOT use shotguns in 2025 when it comes to creative strategy.
Don't just blast dozens of iterations or dozens of videos with small hook changes and just hope
and pray that something hits.

Because that's how people who think “volume is the only answer” test ads, and it's not a
strategy that meta is really allowing or rewarding, especially with its new algorithm.​
​
The shotgun method should only be used for content strategy, and content strategy alone.

### ABO Testing/Scaling Campaign (Recommended)

#1 - Breakthroughs get their own ad set to scale further

#2 - Spend Winners get their own ad set to throttle their spend

#3 - KPI winners get their own ad set to see if they can take on more spend

^ NEVER TURN OFF ANY OF THESE ADS IN THE MAIN CBO AFTER YOU DUPLICATE TO ABO (unless obviously performance is terrible, which is another video)

#4 - Losers will move to [zombie campaign](https://www.skool.com/evolve-8484/classroom/fc758841?md=4c993c9e61e2498f93f575a1dd102c80 "https://www.skool.com/evolve-8484/classroom/fc758841?md=4c993c9e61e2498f93f575a1dd102c80")

These ad sets will have the same setup as the CBO ad sets

### #2 - What budget do you start these at?

5-20% of total daily budget → remember you can always scale up (lower on initial spend on kpi winners, higher initial spend on breakthroughs)

### #3 - Are we still using a champions ad set?

No.

### ABO Testing Only Campaign (Optional)

Again, a separate campaign is OPTIONAL for this. We are currently just adding these as individual adsets in our main testing/scaling ABO campaign.

Assuming the main CBO campaign is running on 7-day click attribution:

**Ad Set 1: Hard Exclusions**

Test the top ads again under 7-day click, but go very aggressive on exclusions. Exclude website visitors, add-to-carts, page engagers (365 days), past customers — basically every possible interaction a user might have with the brand

The goal here is to force Facebook to go after net new people instead of spending on existing audience segments, since the main campaign is already doing that.

**Ad Set 2: 1 Day Click Attribution (Testing OR Scaling)**

Test the same top ads under 1-day click attribution, but with more relaxed exclusions (only exclude purchasers).

1DC is naturally more conservative and tends to limit over-attribution, so we don’t need to be as aggressive with exclusions here.

**Ad Set 3: Incremental Attribution (Testing OR Scaling)**

Test the same top ads using incremental attribution settings, again with very strong exclusions to push Facebook toward net new acquisition.

**IN FINAL**

This isn’t our core campaign.

The main CBO is still cruising and protecting baseline revenue. So even if one of these aggressive tests breaks, the overall business performance won’t break with it.

This structure lets us push growth without destabilizing the foundation.

How To Set Up a 3:2:2 Campaign

32:29

**Easy A UTM code:**

utm\_source=facebook&utm\_medium=paid&utm\_campaign={{ [campaign.name](http://campaign.name/ "http://campaign.name") }}&utm\_term={{ [adset.name](http://adset.name/ "http://adset.name") }}&utm\_content={{ [ad.name](http://ad.name/ "http://ad.name") }}&fbadid={{ [ad.id](http://ad.id/ "http://ad.id") }}&tw\_source={{site\_source\_name}}&tw\_adid={{ [ad.id](http://ad.id/ "http://ad.id") }}  
  
To Sum Up:  
1 Campaign  
1 Champions Ad set, Multiple Creative Test Ad Sets  
3 creatives in each ad set (same concept), each with 2 headlines and 2 copies  
  
**⚠ WARNING DO NOT TURN OFF YOUR EXISTING ADS IF THEY ARE WORKING. THE DUMBEST THING YOU CAN DO IS TURN OFF ADS THAT ARE WORKING. IF YOUR OTHER CAMPAIGNS ARE WORKING - LEAVE THEM ON - IF YOU TURN THEM OFF AND YOU LOSE MONEY... THAT IS ON YOU.**  
  
**FAQ:**

1. **How many creative ad sets can you launch at a time?**
	- If you're only spending few hundred a day, I recommend keeping it to 5 ad sets (including champions ad set, if it has ads inside) at a time so budget can be distributed efficiently. Focus more on intent of the ads instead of throwing things at the wall.
		- You can launch all 5 ad sets in the same day if you wish
		- **If you want the exact calculation on how to figure out the max number of creatives to test per week:**
		Example #1 - Budget $100/day
		Total Budget (example $100/day)
		Target CPA (Example $20)
		Total Ads = Budget/TCPA
		$100/$20 = 5
		So the maximum amount of concepts is 5, considering 100% of your budget is testing.
		- **If you're spending a few thousand a day you can definitely have more ad sets due to budget and CPA**
2. **Do we set min/max spend limits for this campaign method?**
	- No, we tested it and it didn't help.
3. **How long do you test creatives for?**
	- We recommend testing creatives for a minimum of 3 days. However, we now leave them testing 7 days. If they don't do well after 7 days (not much spending or missing KPI), we will then turn them off. If you want to test ads longer, you can. But we usually don't recommend leaving ads on for longer than 14 days.
4. **How do you know if a creative ad set is a winner (Covered in "When To Leave a 3:2:2 Running" video below)?**
	- If it gets majority of spend at KPI like shown in the video. Besides that if you're still not sure, the question you should ask is "Did my overall campaign performance improve?". If your overall campaign ROAS didn't improve, it's not a winner.
5. **When do you turn off creatives/ad sets (Covered in "When To Turn Off A 3:2:2" video below)?**
	- With this method we are only turning off at the ad set level not ad level.
		- If the ad set is still not getting much spend after 7 days, and ROAS is not good we will turn off. If ROAS is good you may leave it running if you have no other creatives in queue.
		- If the ad set took majority of spend within the first 3 days, performance is not good and overall campaign performance went down, we will turn off. But we will do learnings on the ad because something about it got a lot of engagement, so make it more conversion driven. Try to give it 2-3 days to test, it may not convert that well 1st day but 2nd or 3rd day pick up.
		- No we don't turn off ads that were doing well but suddenly tanked, zoom out look at averages instead
6. **What's the minimum budget to launch this?**
	- Minimum I would start this campaign at is $100/day
7. **What if I don't have any past winners to put in the champions ad set or it's a new brand**
	- You can set up the champions ad set still just don't launch any ads inside there until you have a proven winner you can drop inside.
8. **Can you mix images and videos in this same campaign?**
	- Yes, that is fine but keep them separate ad sets. 1 concept should be 1 ad set, so 1 image concept with 3 variations.
