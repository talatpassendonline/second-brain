Produces one deep market-research north-star document (aim 25+ pages) for any brand, emotion-based, with mandatory source links on every claim. Use once per brand as the foundation all copy hangs on. Enable Deep Research, expect 5 to 10 minutes, and use the built-in "Continue" protocol if it hits a length limit.

```text
This is an in-depth market research task. Take your time (5-10 minutes) and produce a highly
detailed report (aim for 25+ pages of content). Quality and depth over speed. Build a market
research document for [BRAND/PRODUCT] using an emotion-based approach that identifies scalable
advertising opportunities. First ask me for the brand website or product info.

CRITICAL: SOURCE LINKS REQUIREMENT. Every quote, statistic, claim, or insight must link to its
source (competitor pages, specific Amazon listings/reviews, exact Reddit threads, social posts,
studies). Format as proper markdown hyperlinks. If you cannot link something, say so and why.

CONTINUATION: if you hit a generation limit, end with "I've reached a generation limit. Please
reply with 'Continue'." On Continue, never restart or repeat, pick up exactly where you left off,
start with "Continuing from [section]...", and append to the artifact rather than rebuilding it.

Work through these steps:
STEP 1 Brand & Product Analysis: the desire/problem it addresses, superior product vs identity
  marketing, mechanisms, positioning vs direct and indirect competitors, price, voice and tone
  (10+ examples), 20+ key benefit/problem phrases.
STEP 2 Desire & Problem Mapping: primary desires driving the market, urgency (1-10), problems
  blocking the desire, emotional state, 5-7 verbatim quotes per desire with sources.
STEP 3 Competitor & Market Research: 10-15 direct competitors (hooks, visuals, longest-running
  ads, pricing, trust signals) and 7-10 categories of indirect/alternative solutions.
STEP 4 Identity Markers (if identity marketing): values, visual markers, language, influencers,
  media, aspirations, opposing identities.
STEP 5 Amazon Research: 40+ direct review quotes on problems, failed prior solutions, a-ha
  moments, objections, timelines, with links.
STEP 6 Reddit Research: 10-15 subreddits, 50+ direct quotes on desires, failed attempts,
  objections, "holy grail" descriptions, with thread links.
STEP 7 Social Media Research: analyze 20-25 winning hooks (by pattern: size, speed, authority,
  before/after, removing limitations, question, newness, exclusivity, belief-challenging,
  direct call-out), visual approaches (benefit/problem/product/product-in-action), 100+ comments,
  influencers, content structures (Symptom->Problem->Solution->Product->Offer down to Offer only).
STEP 8 Market Sophistication (level 1-5 with evidence) and the right positioning strategy.
STEP 9 Ad Style Analysis: UGC vs professional, emotional vs logical, bright vs dark side, short
  vs long, with 10+ examples each.

THROUGHOUT, document: top 30 desire statements, top 30 problem statements, top 20 adaptable
hooks, 15-20 objections with rebuttals, sophistication level, awareness level, top 10 visual
proof elements, top 10 belief mechanisms, 20+ buzz phrases, 15+ success-story patterns.

FINAL: answer the desire/product, audience, and belief/buying-behavior question banks, then
develop at least 5 ad concepts for NEW customers (concept name, positioning, format, avatar,
hook variations, visual concept, structure, script, proof, CTA, objection handling, why it works).
Conclude with a strategic analysis (opportunity, positioning, message, channel, roadmap).
```
