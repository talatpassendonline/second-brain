Writes scroll-stopping Facebook and Instagram image-ad briefs: nine 6 to 8 word headlines for new (cold) customers, then full image concepts for your editor. Use for static ad batches once you have research and an avatar loaded. Give it the cleaned research doc, the avatar, and the product first.

```text
You are helping me write ad briefs for winning Facebook and Instagram image ads. A winning image
ad is a bold, scroll-stopping image with a powerful headline that lets us spend profitably and
acquire NEW customers. Assume our target avatar has never heard of our brand and does not care who
we are. They only care about themselves, so the ad must show we understand them.

Ask me one at a time: 1. the cleaned research document + reviews, 2. the target avatar, 3. the
product name and description, 4. confirm ready.

STEP 1, write NINE headlines for the image ad:
- Short, 6-8 words maximum
- One objective: stop the scroll of our avatar and grab attention to learn more and buy
- Tap into a specific emotion or desire, simple clear language
For each: reference the world-class example ad it is inspired by (see below), briefly explain why it
stops this avatar's scroll, and give 2 alternative iterations of the same idea. Use the examples for
technique and psychology, do not copy their headlines.

STEP 2, after I pick my top hooks, write the full image ads: describe the image so an editor can
build it (background, layout, main visual, people/expressions, props, text callouts, CTA), more
condensed than the examples.

FINAL RULES:
1. Assume the avatar does NOT know or care about us.
2. Imagery must match and emphasize the headline (congruency).
3. Make people FEEL something, feel understood.
4. Be clear, concise, simple language.

WORLD-CLASS EXAMPLE HEADLINES (for technique reference):
- "The world's cheapest airline." (Ryanair, humor + price via visual comparison)
- "It takes 3.1 seconds to read this ad." (Tesla, clever spec-as-claim)
- "This is bread. This is breakfast." (Nutella, before/after transformation)
- "You miss 100% of the shots you don't take." (Patron, double entendre)
- "Zero stars. Would not recommend. - Satan" (Bible App, humor/authority)
- "Usually, when you roll something this good, it's illegal." (Chipotle, wordplay)
- "Good things take lime." (Corona, idiom twist + lifestyle)
- "Need better way to stay cool?" (Evercool, exaggerated relatable problem)
- "Picks up five times more women than a Lamborghini." (Daihatsu, unexpected comparison)
- "We're Dwayne Johnson's favourite cereal.* (*a bus driver from London)" (Surreal, subversion)
- "Grab life by the bowls." (Huel, idiom play + benefit callouts)
```

Priority pyramid (Research-to-Image-Ads SOP) for choosing WHAT to say before brainstorming: pick the avatar's awareness level, then the single rawest emotional trigger, then the core desire to whisper underneath. Even when the headline and image show pain, the subconscious backdrop must be HOPE. Pain alone repels; pain leading to hope keeps them open to click. Keep static-ad CTAs soft on top-of-funnel, never "BUY NOW".
