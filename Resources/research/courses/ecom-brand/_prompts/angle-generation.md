Generates 5 to 7 testable angles against a winning desire using the Belief-Gap-Bridge framework and four tiers of new information. Use after you know which desire converts and need distinct belief-shifts to test, not just louder pain. Feeds contrast-check directly.

```text
I have a winning mass desire and a product. Generate 5-7 different angles to test against that
desire, so I can find which belief-shift converts highest.

ATTACH: Macro Avatar Document (and Macro Avatar Language Document if you have it).
Winning Mass Desire: [the desire that converted in testing]
Product: [what you are selling]

WHAT IS AN ANGLE:
An angle is the new information we give prospects about their problem that makes them finally
take action. It makes them believe our product is the only way to solve their desire, possible
only through our unique mechanism. It is NOT describing pain more dramatically. It is new
information that shifts what they believe about their problem, making the solution feel necessary
and urgent.

THE 4 TIERS OF NEW INFORMATION (power hierarchy):
Tier 1 - Reframe What They're Already Seeing: they observe something daily but misunderstand it.
  "Your dog isn't slowing from old age, they're in chronic pain you can't see."
  Strongest: verifiable right now in their own life, no trust required first.
Tier 2 - Explain Why Their Attempts Failed: they tried things that did not work but don't know
  the real reason. Removes self-blame, creates relief ("it wasn't me, the method was flawed").
Tier 3 - Reveal The Hidden Mechanism: they know the problem but not the real cause. Explains the
  "why" behind the struggle, opens the door to a different solution.
Tier 4 - Create Awareness of Hidden Problems: they don't even know this is happening. Creates
  new urgency around invisible damage happening right now.

BELIEF-GAP-BRIDGE FRAMEWORK:
Step 1 - Understand belief structure: extract from the research what the avatar currently
  believes about their problem, solutions, themselves, and timing.
Step 2 - Find the gap: identify what the avatar DOESN'T know (using the 4 tiers) that would
  change their behavior if they knew it.
Step 3 - Build the bridge: construct 5-7 complete angles that bridge current belief to the new
  information, creating the "aha moment" that drives action.

For each angle, include:
- What they currently believe
- The new information that contradicts it
- How this reframes their understanding
- What they will naturally conclude
- Why this belief-shift works for this specific avatar
- Which tier it uses
```
