Discovers the desires that already exist in a market from hundreds of real customer voices, and separates true desires (outcomes people GET) from product requirements (what they want it to BE). Use as the very first step for a new brand, with Deep Research enabled (Claude Sonnet 4.5 or Gemini Pro). It is step 1 of the 12-module GOD TIER pipeline.

```text
I am researching the mass desires that exist in the market for products in the [PRODUCT CATEGORY]
space in [COUNTRY].
My product category is: [PRODUCT / PRODUCT CATEGORY]
For reference, here is my product: [LINK]. Similar product on Amazon: [AMAZON LINK].

YOUR MISSION: Conduct systematic market research to discover what desires ALREADY EXIST in
this market. Do not assume who the customer is or what they care about. Let the data reveal the
desires, and let the desires reveal the customer. You are a reporter, not a novelist. Report
what the market says, not what you think they should say.

CRITICAL DISTINCTION, WHAT IS A DESIRE:
Desires are outcomes people GET BY USING the product (health outcomes, behavioral changes,
problems solved/prevented, experiences, transformations, financial outcomes).
Desires are NOT product requirements: not "easy to clean", "stainless steel", "BPA-free",
"quiet", "durable". Those describe what the product should BE.
THE FUNDAMENTAL TEST: "Is this something they GET BY USING the product, or something they
want the product TO BE?" GET BY USING = desire. TO BE = product requirement.

DELIVERABLE:
- Top 5 Primary Surface Desires
- Top 5 Secondary/Untapped Desires (find these by researching the product's MECHANISMS, not the
  category: search the adjacent problem spaces where your product type shows up unexpectedly)
- For EACH of the 10 surface desires, find EXACTLY 3 emotional drivers with frequency %. 30 total.
  Non-negotiable. Do not stop until all 10 are mapped.
Format each desire as: "I want [outcome that happens BY USING the product]". One desire = one
outcome. Max 10 words. No "and"/"or" joining multiple wants.

RESEARCH SCOPE (go as deep as the data allows):
- Amazon: 100-200+ reviews across 10-15 competing products, full 1 to 5 star range. Look for
  "would be perfect if...", "I wish...", "disappointed that...", "exactly what I needed because...".
- Reddit: 50-100+ posts and comment threads across 5-10 subreddits (Top all-time, Top year, Recent).
- Forums & Quora: 30-50+ threads. Social: 50+ posts. Review sites/blogs: 20-30+.
Total target: 700-1000+ distinct customer voices. Keep a running count by platform. Note where
you hit saturation (stopped finding new desires). Strong research = top desires repeat dozens of
times across diverse platforms with consistent emotional language.

For each desire track: frequency count, platform distribution, 3-5 verbatim sample quotes,
context notes.
```
