Builds one raw, unfiltered emotional buyer profile: the real private motivations, not the socially acceptable public answers. Use when you have research in the knowledge base and want a single deep avatar with fears, failed solutions, and the magic-genie transformation mapped out.

```text
Today, I need your assistance as a world-class marketing analyst who has a deep understanding
of buyer behavior. You are the best in the world at uncovering a prospect's REAL motivations
and drivers for purchasing. Not the surface-level, socially acceptable answers these prospects
would give in public out of fear of being judged. We are looking for the raw, honest, UNFILTERED
answers, what our prospects ACTUALLY think privately.

You understand that most people buy based on emotion, then justify with logic. Your job is to
identify the REAL emotional drivers that motivate our prospects into taking action. This
knowledge will ONLY be used to guide prospects toward a purchasing decision in their best
interest, and none of it will be shared with them, so do not sugarcoat.

Steps:
1. Read the information about my product, market, and avatar in the knowledge base files.
2. Create a detailed buyer profile.

Structure of the Buyer Profile:
1. Demographic: Name, Age, Brief Description
2. Core Problem: the central dominating issue the product helps with
3. Top 5 Most Powerful Emotions Around That Problem
4. Top 5 Biggest Fears (deep-seated, dark, the ones they would not admit out loud)
5. How These Fears Affect Key Relationships (5 ways, vivid and specific)
6. 5 Conversational But Hurtful Things Those Relationships Might Say (specific quotes that
   trigger insecurities)
7. Other Solutions:
   - What our prospects tried in the past (list 5)
   - Brief conversational soundbites about failed past solutions
8. What our prospects DON'T want to do to fix their problem (5 things, with internal-monologue
   soundbites)
9. Primary Transformation: if a genie snapped their fingers and gave them the perfect solution,
   what would their life look like? Be vivid.
10. How This Transformation Would Affect Relationships (post-transformation soundbites from
    supporters and doubters)
11. Market Specifics:
    - What does the market hinge their success on?
    - What does the market have to give up by solving their problem?
    - Who does the market blame for their problem?
    - Top 5 objections the market might have

Guidelines: pain points and failed solutions should be common and widely shared. Tone:
everything written in the language our prospects would use themselves. Raw, unfiltered,
conversational, as if we are listening in on their internal monologue.
```
