## Door 1, sound-off tekst-story (stille scroller in de feed)

**Hook (kaart 1, groot leesbaar):** "If you gave up on calcium because 'the experts say tablets are useless'. Read this before you give up for good."

**Tekstbeats (kaart per regel):**
1. Professor Tim Spector said it plainly: there is no good evidence that artificial calcium tablets lower your fracture risk.
2. He is right. But here is the line the headlines never finished.
3. The same experts, and the Royal Osteoporosis Society, then tell you to get your calcium from food.
4. Their point was never "give up." It was "stop relying on the chalk tablet, and get it from food."
5. Nobody finished that sentence for you.
6. Goatly is calcium as food. One ingredient: pure goat milk, gently dried. No chalk, no tablet your gut fights.

**CTA-kaart:** "Read why food calcium is different →"
**Visual:** schone tekstkaarten, hoog contrast, grote serif of strak sans, leesbaar zonder geluid. Slotbeeld: een hand met een korrelig wit tabletje naast een lepel geitenmelkpoeder. 4:5, tekst in safe zone. Generiek tablet (geen merk).

**Hook (gesproken + caption):** "I am 63 and I quit calcium tablets two years ago. Turns out I quit for the right reason, but I drew the wrong conclusion."

**Script:**
"I kept reading that calcium supplements are useless. Spector, the ZOE lot, all of them. So I stopped taking mine. What none of them spelled out, and I only worked out later, is that they are talking about the tablets. The chalky ones. The advice sitting right underneath was always: get your calcium from food. I just never did anything with it. This is goat milk powder. One ingredient. It is calcium as a food, not a tablet sitting in my stomach. One spoon in my tea every morning. That is the whole thing."

**Visual:** echte-ogende vrouw ~60, eigen keuken, handheld, geen studio, geen perfecte belichting. Ondertitels ingebrand (sound-off). Toont kort de zak (referentie-afbeelding, maat ankeren ~20cm). 9:16.

## Door 3, authority talking head (verpleegkundige, Nadine-equivalent)

**Hook (gesproken):** "If you are a woman over 50 who has lost faith in calcium tablets, give me two minutes. I am a nurse."

**Script:**
"You have seen the headlines that calcium supplements do not work. They are not wrong. What they mean is the artificial tablet. A big dose of carbonate, more than your body can take in at once, so a lot of it sits in the gut and feels like concrete. The part that matters is what those same experts say next: get calcium from food, where it arrives in small, steady amounts your body actually absorbs. Goat milk is one of those foods. Goatly is simply that, dried into a powder. One ingredient. It is not another supplement. It is food."

**Visual:** kalme vrouw 50-60 in klinische/huiselijke setting, recht in camera, lower-third "registered nurse" (presenter, geen echte persoon nadoen). Rustig tempo. 9:16, caption ingebrand.
