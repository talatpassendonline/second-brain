## Door 1, sound-off tekst-story (stille scroller in de feed)

**Hook (kaart 1):** "If you take a calcium tablet every day and assume your bones are covered, here is the word the bone specialists use for it."

**Tekstbeats:**
1. The specialists who recommend a calcium tablet have a word for it: an adjunct.
2. Adjunct means a top-up. A supporting player. Never the treatment on its own.
3. You were handed the sidekick and told it was the hero.
4. So when your scan did not improve, it was not your body failing the calcium.
5. You had built the whole house on a top-up.
6. Bone is built from food. Goatly is calcium as food: one ingredient, pure goat milk, the actual material, not a tablet propping things up.

**CTA-kaart:** "See what 'adjunct' really means →"
**Visual:** tekstkaarten, het woord "ADJUNCT" groot uitgelicht, dan een lepel geitenmelkpoeder. Hoog contrast, sound-off leesbaar. 4:5.

## Claims-basis (ter verificatie, jouw compliance-call)

- Women's Health Concern: calcium geclassificeerd als adjunct, niet zelfstandige behandeling. Bron in [[uk-angles]] A1 (Evidence: Strong).
- "Adjunct/top-up" wordt geframed als klinische classificatie, niet als letterlijke tekst op een specifiek doosje (blijf weg van merknamen, Meta + accuratesse).
- Geen genezings-/omkeerclaim, geen verzonnen getallen. Veilige botclaim indien nodig op de advertorial: "Calcium is needed for the maintenance of normal bones."
