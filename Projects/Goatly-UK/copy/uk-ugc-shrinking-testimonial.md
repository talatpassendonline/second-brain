# UGC Testimonial — "Shrinking"

**Format:** Phone-camera UGC. British woman, 60–68. Kitchen or sitting room, natural light. Relaxed, unhurried. She is not performing.

**Destination link:** Advertorial (not PDP).

---

## Structure

Fixed body (identical under each hook). Hook only swaps (~3 sec).

---

## Hooks (test separately)

**Hook A — Primary:**
"My doctor told me I was shrinking. I'd been taking calcium tablets the whole time."

**Hook B:**
"I stopped taking my calcium tablets. I never told my doctor."

**Hook C:**
"I threw away half a packet of calcium tablets last month. Best thing I've done in years."

**[0:04 – 0:14]**

I'd taken them for years. Every morning. Chalky white tablet, glass of water. My last scan came back no better than the one before, and I thought, well. What was the point of all that.

**[0:14 – 0:27]**

Then someone told me something that stuck. Apparently those tablets are basically just chalk. One mineral on its own, and your body can hardly absorb it. Calcium from food is different. It comes with everything it needs to actually get to your bones.

**[0:27 – 0:37]**

So I found this. Goatly. Pure goat milk powder, one ingredient. One scoop in my morning tea. Ten seconds. No more pills.

**[0:37 – 0:47]**

Within a fortnight my stomach had settled. Mornings feel a bit easier. Small things. But real ones.

**[0:47 – 0:58]**

Link's below. Have a read before you order another pack of those tablets. There's a ninety-day money-back, even if the pouch is empty.

## Production Notes

- No text overlays on the hook line. Let her say it into silence. The first four seconds only work if nothing competes.
- The "apparently" in the mechanism section should land with a natural pause before it, as if she's retrieving a fact from memory, not reading it off a card. That hesitation is what makes it feel real rather than scripted.
- Link in CTA goes to the advertorial, not the product page. This is top-of-funnel.

## Claim Scope

- Stomach settled within a fortnight: [[goat milk]] is A2 protein, gentler on older digestion.
- Mornings easier: experiential, not a claim of reversal.
- "Doesn't say worse" on scan: omitted in final draft as too clinical for this tone.
