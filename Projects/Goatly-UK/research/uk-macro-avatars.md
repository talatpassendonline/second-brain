# SECTION 1: DETAILED RESEARCH FINDINGS

## Macro-Avatar #1: The Independence Guardian, "I want to stay independent as I age"
**Evidence strength: MEDIUM.** Emotion verified (Prompt 1). Direct first-person UK "don't want to be a burden" voice is thin, flagged.

**TRIGGER (most common):** a fragility fracture or a DEXA result that makes "I could end up needing care" suddenly real. UK pathway: a fall and break routes her to a fracture-clinic DEXA. Healthtalk: an osteoporosis nurse rang Iris after a fall, *"I understand that you've had a break recently. I wonder if we could invite you in for a Dexa scan."*

**BEHAVIOURS (thin, honest):** takes prescribed calcium+D, weighs up bisphosphonates with fear of side-effects, researches on forums (HealthUnlocked, Mumsnet). No rich independent behaviour corpus survived verification for this desire specifically.

**LIMITING BELIEFS:**
- *"It is just part of getting older."* ROS confirms this is widespread: *"there's an ill-informed perception that osteoporosis is an unavoidable consequence of women getting older."* SHATTER: it is preventable, and 1 in 2 women over 50 will break a bone from it. A medical condition, not destiny.
- *"It is too late, the damage is done."* SHATTER (educational, not a product claim): bone protection can start even in the 70s and 80s (Dr Louise Newson).

**AWARENESS:** Solution-Aware to Product-Aware. Fluent in T-scores and FRAX, trusts ROS/NHS/GP over supplement ads.

## Macro-Avatar #2: The Vanishing Frame, "I want to stop shrinking and standing curved"
**Evidence strength: MEDIUM-STRONG on emotion (Prompt 1 verbatim), thin on D2-specific trigger.**

**TRIGGER (most common):** the shock of noticing lost height or a curving back, often at a routine measurement. The single most under-discussed trigger and the most emotionally violent.

**Verbatim (from Prompt 1, Healthtalk/Oxford):**
- *"Losing an inch in height shocked her more than the diagnosis itself"* (Clare).
- *"My normal height was five foot six,"* now four foot six (Joan).
- *"I've got the Kyphosis of the back, the hump back. My shape had gone"* (Victoria Iris).
- *"I felt so, so embarrassed with this awful protruding abdomen"* (Rose, 76).

**BEHAVIOURS (thin):** posture/exercise attempts, dietary calcium, supplements. A HealthUnlocked thread literally titled *"is it unusual to lose lots of height"* exists, so the worry is actively voiced, but no single clean behaviour corpus verified.

**LIMITING BELIEFS:**
- *"It is just ageing, women get shorter."* SHATTER: rapid height loss is a vertebral-fracture signal, not normal ageing, and it is partly preventable.
- *"Nothing can reverse it now."* SHATTER (educational): further loss can be slowed; the goal is to stop the next vertebra going.

**AWARENESS:** Solution-Aware. Knows the words (kyphosis, vertebral fracture) once diagnosed, but often was never told the menopause-bone link before it happened.

## Macro-Avatar #3: The Fracture Sentinel, "I want to prevent my next or first broken bone"
**Evidence strength: STRONG.**

**TRIGGER (most common):** a first fragility fracture, or a DEXA T-score in the osteopenia/osteoporosis band. Verbatim (Mumsnet, UK):
- *"Diagnosed at 48 after fall and fracture,"* low vitamin D, grandmother early hip replacement (Aubree17).
- *"I am -3.3 in one area of my spine, osteopenia in hips,"* with *"sister, mother and grandmother all had it"* (Wishimaywishimight).

**BEHAVIOURS:** considering vs fearing alendronic acid (active HealthUnlocked threads "worried about going on alendronic acid"), taking calcium+D, vitamin D, weighing HRT, forum research.

**LIMITING BELIEFS:**
- *"It is just part of getting older."* SHATTER: *"One in two women over 50 will break a bone because of osteoporosis"* (ROS), and it is preventable.
- *"Osteopenia is a made-up disease, the scans are dodgy."* Voiced verbatim on HealthUnlocked: *"the newly made up disease of osteopenia, made up by the same companies that make the drugs."* SHATTER without dismissing the fear: the FRAX risk is real and measured. This avatar already had real falls and fractures.

**AWARENESS:** Solution-Aware to Product-Aware. Quotes exact T-scores and FRAX percentages.

## Macro-Avatar #4: The Frozen Gardener, "I want to keep gardening and walking without fear"
**Evidence strength: MEDIUM-LOW on direct UK voice.** The fear-limits-activity logic is ROS-verified; the first-person "I just want to garden again" line is the NL dream-outcome, not a harvested UK quote. Flagged honestly.

**TRIGGER (most common):** post-diagnosis fear that the activities she loves will now break her. ROS (verified, Prompt 1): *"fear of broken bones can limit what you feel able to do,"* specifically *"whilst exercising or increasing physical activity."*

**BEHAVIOURS (thin):** withdrawing from or hesitating over gardening, lifting, walking on uneven ground; seeking "safe exercise" guidance.

**LIMITING BELIEFS:**
- *"If I stay still I am safer."* SHATTER: inactivity weakens bone further; weight-bearing movement is protective. The fear is the trap.
- *"It is too late to be active safely."* SHATTER (educational): graded weight-bearing exercise is recommended at every age.

**AWARENESS:** Solution-Aware. Caught between "stay active" (what she is told) and "do not fall" (what she fears).

## Macro-Avatar #5: The Betrayed Pill-Taker, "I want calcium my body actually absorbs"
**Evidence strength: STRONGEST.** This is the best-evidenced avatar across all three passes.

**TRIGGER (most common):** the gut-punch that years of calcium pills did nothing. A worse DEXA, or a fracture, despite faithful supplementing. Verbatim (HealthUnlocked Bone Health):
- *"Ad Cald3 is a complete waste of time. I took it for 15 or 20 years and still slipped into osteoporosis."*
- *"My latest Dexa scan seem to be worse than 3 years ago"* (after 3 years of prescribed AdCal-D3).

**BEHAVIOURS:** abandoning supplements after disappointment, switching carbonate to citrate for the gut, taking vitamin D and K2, increasing dietary calcium, forum research. GI misery is the loud switch-trigger: *"It felt like concrete in my gut,"* *"Calcichew is carbonate which gives my constipation,"* *"nausea, retching up white phlegm, and stomach pains."*

**LIMITING BELIEFS (the richest set):**
- *"I get all the calcium I need from food."* ROS-endorsed verbatim: *"You can usually get all the calcium you need for your bones from your food, without taking a supplement."* SHATTER: the at-risk reader is the exception ROS buries (1,000mg/day if at risk or on bone medicine). For Goatly this belief is an **ally** (Goatly is food).
- *"I take my calcium, so I am covered."* SHATTER: calcium is *"an adjunct to other treatments,"* not a treatment by itself (Women's Health Concern). Taking it does not mean you are covered.
- *"Calcium supplements are useless anyway."* Spector verbatim: *"absolutely no evidence... giving extra calcium in the form of these artificial tablets is going to help your fracture risk,"* a *"myth."* SHATTER/nuance: he means non-deficient general population; trials exclude diagnosed osteoporosis. For Goatly this attacks **pills**, not food.
- *"It is all chalk that hurts my stomach."* The pill misery above. Goatly sidesteps the pill entirely.

**AWARENESS:** Solution-Aware, sceptical, fluent. Trusts ROS, NHS, Spector/ZOE, menopause specialists, and her own forum peers over any supplement brand. Confused about absorption specifically, the one place a food-calcium story can enter.
