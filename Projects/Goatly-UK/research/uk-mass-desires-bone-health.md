UK mass-desire research for [[business|Goatly]] in the **calcium / bone-health / osteoporosis supplement** space, women 50+. Mirror of the NL `Prompt 1 - uitslag.md` framework, adapted to the bone-health buyer (not the goat-milk-category buyer).

## 1. Executive summary

**Bottom line:** the UK 50+ bone market runs on **fear of bodily decline, not a wish for "more calcium."** The dominant register is dread of fracture, grief over visible height loss and a curving spine, and the specific terror of physically *becoming* the mother or aunt who had osteoporosis. This is the exact NL emotional spine, and it **transfers cleanly to the UK**. The menopausal oestrogen drop is the accepted mechanism that makes the fear legitimate and makes peri/post-menopausal women the unambiguous avatar. Goatly's "not more calcium, the right form" wedge is **uncontested white space**: the UK incumbent praises calcium carbonate and never mentions K2, goat milk or A2.

**Sources analysed:** 23 fetched, 98 claims extracted, 25 adversarially verified, **21 confirmed / 4 refuted**. Source mix: UK institutional/charity (ROS, NHS), Oxford DIPEx qualitative archive (Healthtalk, 40 UK home interviews), one menopause-clinician podcast (Dr Louise Newson), brand pages (Vitabiotics Osteocare, St Helen's Farm), plus forum threads (HealthUnlocked Bone Health, Mumsnet menopause) that informed direction but were not count-verified.

**Top primary surface desires (verified, outcome-based):**
1. I want to stay independent as I age
2. I want to stop shrinking and standing curved
3. I want to prevent my next broken bone
4. I want to keep gardening and walking without fear
5. I want calcium my body actually absorbs *(partly verified, see gap note)*

**Top secondary / untapped desires (white space, latent demand to be created):**
6. I want the right form of calcium, not chalk
7. I want a natural, non-hormonal way to protect my bones
8. I want to avoid ending up disabled like my mother
9. I want to keep my normal body shape and waist
10. I want proof my bones are actually improving

**Top emotional drivers:** fear of becoming my mother/aunt, grief over lost height, embarrassment and wounded pride over a changed body shape, fear of the next fracture that shrinks my life, overwhelming anxiety at diagnosis, dread of losing independence and becoming a burden.

**Segments:** 3 plausible clusters emerge (anxious newcomer, grieving long-term sufferer, proactive perimenopausal preventer) but are **hypotheses pending forum validation**, not confirmed.

**Confidence:** HIGH for emotional drivers, mechanism, and competitive white space (multiple unanimous primary UK sources). MEDIUM for the desire *rankings* and LOW for any *count*, because the raw review corpus was not mined. The single most commercially important wedge ("I took calcium for years and it did nothing") is **the least verified with customer voice** and is the priority for the next pass.

## 7. Natural desire-based segments (hypotheses, need forum validation)

> [!question] Not confirmed. These are plausible clusters from the qualitative data, to be validated against a real forum corpus (HealthUnlocked Bone Health, Mumsnet, Reddit).

**Segment A, the anxious newcomer (just diagnosed)**
Core emotion: overwhelming anxiety + fear of the unknown future. Trigger: a recent DEXA / T-score result or first fracture. Behaviour: floods HealthUnlocked and Mumsnet with "newly diagnosed, advice please," asks about Adcal-D3 and alendronic acid, fears the medication. Language: "shocked," "scared," "where do I start."

**Segment B, the grieving long-term sufferer**
Core emotion: grief + wounded pride over irreversible change. Trigger: years of progression, visible height loss and kyphosis. Behaviour: talks about lost height in inches, changed body shape, withdrawing socially. Language: "I've lost three inches," "the hump," "my shape had gone," "embarrassed."

**Segment C, the proactive perimenopausal preventer**
Core emotion: fear-led prevention + desire for control. Trigger: menopause + a mother with osteoporosis. Behaviour: researches before any diagnosis, reads Newson/menopause content, weighs HRT vs natural routes, calcium-diet conscious. Language: "menopause and bones," "before it's too late," "natural," "I don't want HRT."

Goatly's NL avatar (De Versterker, see [[icp]]) maps closest to **B and C combined**.

## 9. Research quality and honest assessment

**Source breakdown:** primary UK institutional/qualitative (ROS, NHS, Oxford/Healthtalk) carried the confirmed claims. Forum threads (HealthUnlocked Bone Health, three Mumsnet menopause/health threads) were fetched and informed direction but did **not** survive into the count-verified set. Two brand pages (Vitabiotics Osteocare, St Helen's Farm) locked the competitive picture.

**Confidence by layer:**
- Emotional drivers + mechanism: **HIGH.** Multiple unanimous primary sources, 3-0 verification on the key claims.
- Competitive white space: **HIGH.** Osteocare praises carbonate, omits K2/goat-milk/A2/right-form. St Helen's Farm is the only "easily absorbed form" voice and it is brand marketing.
- Desire rankings: **MEDIUM.** Directionally sound, not count-saturated.
- Any specific number: **LOW.** Not mined. Do not quote counts as data.

**Desires expected but NOT found with customer voice (the gaps):**
1. **"I took calcium for years and it did nothing."** ✅ **Filled in pass 2** ([[uk-voc-absorption-and-goatmilk]]). Real UK voices: "a complete waste of time, I took it for 20 years and still slipped into osteoporosis." But pass 2 also surfaced a **wedge risk**: ROS tells UK buyers carbonate and citrate are equally good for bones, so the "right form" framing needs a UK pivot toward "food not pills" and GI tolerability.
2. **UK goat-milk perception for adults.** ⚠️ **Still mostly unfilled.** Only one verified adult sensory voice (a yoghurt review). Needs a paid scrape of St Helen's Farm milk/powder reviews.
3. **HRT-rejecter sizing.** How many UK women actively refuse HRT and seek a non-hormonal bone product (Goatly's buyer) is not quantified.

**Surprises:**
1. The UK has a **national charity (ROS) that openly names the emotional toll** (body image, anxiety, fear of fracture). Unlike NL, where Voedingscentrum *disputes* goat milk, the UK authority *validates the fear* you are selling to. Friendlier ground.
2. The incumbent does Goatly a favour: Osteocare **explicitly celebrates calcium carbonate**, handing you the "wrong form / poorly-absorbed chalk" contrast on a plate.
3. The **body-shape / wounded-pride angle** (Rose, Ann) is emotionally loud and entirely unsold-to by clinical supplement brands. Potential differentiated angle.

**Four refuted claims (do NOT use):** UK osteoporosis is "usually found by surprise DEXA" (refuted), "half of women over 50 without HRT get osteoporosis" as a UK stat (refuted), St Helen's Farm "positions goat milk against pills" (refuted, it does not), Osteocare frames itself as whole-family "normal bones" not density (refuted).

**Compliance note:** claims and their sources are laid out so [[me|Talat]] can verify and judge what is usable in UK copy. No compliance verdicts imposed here, per vault rule 16.
