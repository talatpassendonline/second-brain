## Phase 3: Mechanisms

> The Betrayed Pill-Taker is the lead avatar (best-evidenced, the Schwartz play lives here), so it carries the four core mechanisms fully. The other avatars reuse/adapt them to their desire.

### MACRO-AVATAR: THE BETRAYED PILL-TAKER
**Core desire:** "I want calcium my body actually absorbs."

#### UMP/UMS #1: "The Chalk Bottleneck"
- **Tier:** 2 (why previous solutions failed)
- **Limiting belief addressed:** "I take my calcium so I'm covered."
- **Observable proof (their words):** "It felt like concrete in my gut"; "Calcichew is carbonate which gives my constipation"; "a complete waste of time, I took it for 20 years and still slipped into osteoporosis."
- **UMP:** A calcium pill dumps a big slug of chalk into your gut all at once. Your body can only take in a small amount of calcium at a time, so most of it never gets absorbed. It just sits there, irritates your gut (the constipation, the "concrete" feeling), and leaves. You were never short on calcium tablets. You were short on calcium your body could actually take in.
- **UMS:** Goatly is calcium the way it comes in food: carried in milk, in small, steady, usable amounts your body is built to absorb, gentle enough to take every single day. No chalk slug. No concrete gut.
- **Evidence:** **Strong.** Absorption is genuinely capped per dose (the ~500mg ceiling surfaced verbatim in the HealthUnlocked threads); calcium-carbonate GI intolerance is well-documented.
- **Believability check:** connects to what they felt (gut), verifiable in their own experience, repeatable to a friend, no pseudo-science. ✅

#### UMP/UMS #2: "Calcium With No Driver"
- **Tier:** 3 (new mechanism for a familiar problem)
- **Limiting belief addressed:** "I take my calcium so I'm covered" / "the form doesn't matter."
- **Observable proof:** "my Dexa scan seem to be worse than 3 years ago" despite faithfully taking it; "I like that it includes other minerals to aid absorption."
- **UMP:** Calcium on its own doesn't know where to go. It needs vitamin K2 to steer it into your bones instead of letting it drift into your arteries and soft tissue. Here's the kicker: the UK's No.1 calcium pill has no K2 in it at all. You've been swallowing calcium with no driver, so it never reached the one place you needed it.
- **UMS:** Goatly delivers calcium together with its natural co-factors, the team that actually directs it into bone, the way whole food carries them, not a lone mineral with no map.
- **Evidence:** **Moderate.** K2's role in directing calcium is researched but still debated; the verified, undeniable part is that Osteocare's formula contains no vitamin K/K2 (confirmed in [[uk-voc-absorption-and-goatmilk]]). Lead with the competitor gap, soften the biology.
- **Believability check:** the "no driver / no map" analogy is simple and the competitor-omission is checkable on the box. ✅

#### UMP/UMS #3: "Food, Not Pills" (The Adjunct Trap)
- **Tier:** 2 + 3
- **Limiting belief addressed:** "supplements are useless" (Spector/Cooper) and "I get my calcium from food."
- **Observable proof:** the whole "pills did nothing" chorus + the authority noise they've already heard ("Tim Spector says supplements are useless").
- **UMP:** Here's what nobody told you straight: a calcium tablet was never meant to fix your bones. Even your own bone charity says a tablet is only a "top-up", and to get your calcium from food. The famous professors saying "supplements are useless" are right about one thing, the artificial tablets. They're not talking about food. You've been judging the wrong thing.
- **UMS:** Goatly isn't a supplement pill. It's food, real calcium from milk, exactly the source the experts point you to, made into a simple daily routine. It was never a pill problem. It was a food problem.
- **Evidence:** **Strong.** ROS/NHS/BDA all say food-first; WHC says calcium is "adjunct not treatment"; Spector/Cooper attack tablets, not food. This judo-flips the anti-supplement narrative in Goatly's favour.
- **Believability check:** it aligns with what their own trusted authorities already say. Highest believability of the set. ✅✅

#### UMP/UMS #4: "Bricks Without Builders"
- **Tier:** 3
- **Limiting belief addressed:** "I take my calcium so I'm covered."
- **Observable proof:** "wonder if they even make a difference"; taking calcium for years with no result.
- **UMP:** Bone isn't made of calcium alone. It's built by a team, calcium plus phosphorus, magnesium, protein and K2, in the balance they naturally come in milk. Swallowing calcium by itself is like dumping a pile of bricks on a building site with no mortar and no builders. The bricks just sit there.
- **UMS:** Goatly brings the whole team in one scoop of goat milk, calcium with the co-factors it's meant to arrive with, so your body can actually build with it.
- **Evidence:** **Moderate.** The co-factor/ratio story is reasonable and food genuinely carries them; avoid claiming a unique goat-milk superiority.
- **Believability check:** the bricks/builders image is instantly graspable. ✅

### MACRO-AVATAR: THE VANISHING FRAME
**Core desire:** "I want to stop shrinking and standing curved."

#### UMP/UMS #5: "Shrinking Is a Deficit, Not a Birthday"
- **Tier:** 1 (reframe) + 3
- **Limiting belief addressed:** "Women just get shorter with age" / "nothing can reverse it."
- **Observable proof:** "I had no idea I had lost height until my daughter said she's taller than me"; "my tummy sticks out like I'm pregnant"; "losing an inch shocked her more than the diagnosis."
- **UMP:** Getting shorter isn't your age. It's tiny bones in your spine quietly collapsing because they're losing more material than they're being fed. Each lost inch is a vertebra giving way. It's not a birthday, it's a building-material shortage, and the curve and the "pregnant" tummy are what that shortage looks like from the outside.
- **UMS:** Goatly feeds the frame daily with usable, food-form calcium and its team, so the bones in your spine have what they need to hold their shape instead of crumbling inch by inch.
- **Evidence:** **Strong** on the reframe (height loss = vertebral fracture, not normal ageing, is medically true per ROS/NHS); **Moderate** on Goatly halting it (don't promise reversal).
- **Believability check:** ties their visible, frightening change to a simple cause. ✅

- Reuse #3, framed for the frame: "you've taken calcium for years and still shrank, because a chalk tablet was never reaching the spine. Real food calcium is a different thing." **Strong.**

## Final analysis

### Research insights
- **Most frequent avatar/desire in research:** the Betrayed Pill-Taker by a distance, the "pills did nothing" disappointment is everywhere. The Vanishing Frame (height/shape) is the most emotionally raw.
- **Strongest knowledge gap:** that a calcium tablet was never a treatment (only an "adjunct") and that absorption is capped, almost no one knows this. Big "oh THAT'S why" potential.
- **Strongest limiting belief to break:** "I take my calcium so I'm covered." It is the false security that keeps them on the pills that fail them.

### Mechanism strength rankings
- **Most observable proof:** #1 Chalk Bottleneck and #3 Food Not Pills (their own gut + their own authorities).
- **Strongest aha moment:** #2 Calcium With No Driver (the No.1 pill has no K2) and #1 Chalk Bottleneck.
- **Most directly breaks the key belief:** #3 Food Not Pills (kills "supplements are useless" by making Goatly food, not a supplement).
- **Easiest to believe/understand:** #3, then #1, then #9 Stillness Is the Trap.
- **Best evidence base:** #3, #1, #9, #7 (all Strong). Handle #2, #4, #10 with the softer framing flagged.

### Testing recommendations
1. **Start here:** #3 "Food, Not Pills" on the **Betrayed Pill-Taker**. Highest believability, breaks the strongest belief, and rides the UK anti-supplement narrative instead of fighting it. This is the lead hook.
2. **Second:** #1 "The Chalk Bottleneck" (same avatar) for the visceral gut proof.
3. **Combine for power:** #3 + #1 in one piece, *"it was never a pill problem (food not pills), and here's why the chalk never reached you (the bottleneck)."* That's a full Tier-2-into-Tier-3 lead.
4. **Emotional scaler:** #10 "Becoming Your Mother Is an Input" for the Independence/Frame avatars once a desire wins, it carries the heaviest emotion.
5. **Avoid leading with:** any "goat milk uniquely beats cow milk for bones" claim (thin science). Keep goat milk as the clean, gentle, whole-food carrier, not the magic.
