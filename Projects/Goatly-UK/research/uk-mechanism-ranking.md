## MACRO-AVATAR #1: THE BETRAYED PILL-TAKER
**Core desire:** "I want calcium my body actually absorbs."
**Emotion:** distrust / betrayal. **Belief:** "I take my calcium so I'm covered." **Trigger:** worse DEXA or a fracture despite years of faithful pills.

**RANKING:**

**1. "Food, Not Pills" (The Adjunct Trap)**
Why #1: Highest belief-shift and highest believability. It kills the exact belief that defines this avatar ("I'm covered because I take calcium") by revealing a tablet was never a treatment, only an adjunct, and it does so using the authorities she already trusts (ROS, NHS, Spector). It converts her existing cynicism ("supplements are useless") into a reason to buy, because Goatly is food, not a supplement. Best competitive differentiation (it's not even in the pill category), and it validates the betrayal she feels: "you judged the wrong thing, not your own body." Perfect awareness-fit for a solution-aware, disappointed buyer.

**2. "The Chalk Bottleneck"**
Why #2: Strongest observable proof of the whole set. She has literally felt this ("concrete in my gut", constipation, the chalky pill). It explains the past failure viscerally and physically, which lands hard for someone whose trigger was "the pill did nothing." Slightly behind #1 only because #1 breaks the core belief more completely; combine them and you have the lead.

**3. "Calcium With No Driver" (K2)**
Why #3: Strongest pure "aha" and great differentiation (the No.1 UK pill has no K2). But it's a notch more technical (lower on simplicity), and the K2 biology is moderate-evidence, so it works best as a supporting reveal after #1/#2, not the opening.

**4. "Bricks Without Builders"**
Why #4: Simple and believable, but it overlaps heavily with #3 and carries less unique observable proof she can verify in her own body. A useful reinforcing image, weakest as a standalone lead for her.

## MACRO-AVATAR #2: THE VANISHING FRAME
**Core desire:** "I want to stop shrinking and standing curved."
**Emotion:** grief + wounded pride. **Belief:** "Women just get shorter with age / nothing reverses it." **Trigger:** the shock of noticing lost height.

**RANKING:**

**1. "Shrinking Is a Deficit, Not a Birthday"**
Why #1: Built for her. It attacks her exact belief ("it's just ageing") and explains her most distressing, visible experience, the lost inches, the curve, the "pregnant" tummy, as one clear cause (vertebrae giving way from a building-material shortfall). Highest emotional resonance with her grief and wounded pride, and high observable proof (she can see and measure the shrink). Strong evidence on the reframe (height loss = vertebral fracture, not normal ageing).

**2. "Food, Not Pills"**
Why #2: Explains why the calcium she has taken did not stop the shrinking, and gives her a believable new route. Strong belief-shift and believability, just less emotionally tied to the frame than #1.

**3. "The Chalk Bottleneck"**
Why #3: Good mechanical explanation of why the pill never reached her spine, but it speaks to the gut more than to the frame, so it resonates less with her specific grief.

**4. "Bricks Without Builders"**
Why #4: Fine and simple, but generic for her, no special pull on the height/shape wound.

## MACRO-AVATAR #3: THE FRACTURE SENTINEL
**Core desire:** "I want to prevent my next or first broken bone."
**Emotion:** fear of the next break + diagnosis anxiety. **Belief:** "It's just part of getting older / osteopenia is a made-up disease." **Trigger:** first fracture or an osteoporosis-band DEXA.

**RANKING:**

**1. "One in Two Isn't Fate, It's a Shortfall"**
Why #1: Directly reframes her fatalism ("just getting older") using the 1-in-2 stat she has already seen, and turns dread into something fixable, which speaks straight to her fear and her need for reassurance. High emotional resonance (fear) and strong evidence (menopause-bone-loss is verified). It sidesteps a head-on fight with "osteopenia is made-up" by focusing on the shortfall, not the label.

**2. "The Chalk Bottleneck"**
Why #2: For the Sentinel already on AdCal/Calcichew and still fracturing ("the calcium has not stopped me fracture"), this is the visceral explanation of why her protection failed. Very strong observable proof; ranks just under #1 because #1 better addresses the fear-of-fate belief.

**3. "Food, Not Pills"**
Why #3: Strong belief-shift and validates abandoning the pills, but for a fear-driven buyer who wants protection now, the reframe in #1 and the proof in #2 land faster.

**4. "Calcium With No Driver"**
Why #4: The aha is real but more technical; a frightened, anxious buyer wants reassurance more than biochemistry, so this is a later-funnel detail for her.

## MACRO-AVATAR #4: THE FROZEN GARDENER
**Core desire:** "I want to keep gardening and walking without fear."
**Emotion:** fear that activity will fracture her + grief over a shrinking life. **Belief:** "If I stay still I'm safer." **Trigger:** post-diagnosis fear of the things she loves.

**RANKING:**

**1. "Stillness Is the Trap"**
Why #1: Built for her, and the only mechanism that flips her core belief head-on ("staying still is safer" becomes "stillness weakens bone faster, making a break more likely"). It resolves the exact tension she lives in (told to stay active, terrified to), with strong evidence (disuse weakens bone) and high emotional resonance (her shrinking world). The clear winner.

**2. "Food, Not Pills"**
Why #2: Pairs naturally with #1, the usable building material that lets her keep moving with confidence. Strong support mechanism, strong believability.

**3. "The Chalk Bottleneck"**
Why #3: Explains why her pills are not protecting her enough to feel safe, useful, but one step removed from the activity-fear that defines her.

**4. "Bricks Without Builders"**
Why #4: Generic for her; least pull on the fear-of-movement belief.

## MACRO-AVATAR #5: THE INDEPENDENCE GUARDIAN
**Core desire:** "I want to stay independent as I age."
**Emotion:** fear of becoming a burden. **Belief:** "It's just getting older / it's too late, the damage is done." **Trigger:** a fall or DEXA that makes "I could need care" real.

**RANKING:**

**1. "Becoming Your Mother Is an Input, Not Your Genes"**
Why #1: Hits the deepest fear this avatar carries (becoming the disabled mother) and reframes it from inherited fate to a correctable shortfall, breaking both her beliefs ("just ageing" and the implied "it's in my family") at once. Highest emotional resonance of any mechanism for any avatar. Evidence is moderate, so frame as "not destiny" rather than "genes don't matter," but the pull is enormous.

**2. "It's Not Too Late, the Bone Is Still Listening"**
Why #2: Directly dismantles her timing belief ("it's too late, the damage is done") with the true, simple idea that bone renews itself for life. Pairs cleanly with #1 (you can still break the pattern).

**3. "Food, Not Pills"**
Why #3: Gives her a believable, sustainable daily input she can actually keep up for years, and validates that the pills others rely on were never the answer. Strong, but less emotionally central than the becoming-her frame.

**4. "One in Two Isn't Fate, It's a Shortfall"**
Why #4: Reinforces the fixable-not-fate theme, but it's pitched at fracture-fear more than at the independence/burden fear that defines her, so it supports rather than leads.

## Overall read (cross-avatar)

- **The single strongest mechanism across the board is "Food, Not Pills"** (top-2 for every avatar). It is the brand-level spine.
- **"The Chalk Bottleneck"** is the strongest proof-mechanism and the best partner to pair with Food Not Pills.
- **Each avatar then has ONE tailor-made winner** that should lead its specific ad: Frame → "Deficit Not a Birthday", Sentinel → "One in Two Isn't Fate", Gardener → "Stillness Is the Trap", Guardian → "Becoming Your Mother Is an Input".
- **Recommended test order:** (1) Betrayed Pill-Taker with "Food, Not Pills" + "Chalk Bottleneck" combined, the highest-confidence, best-evidenced lead. Then (2) the Vanishing Frame with "Deficit Not a Birthday", the most emotionally raw. Validate the winning desire before scaling into the others.
