**About the problem**
- Thinks causes it: "It's just my age. It's in my family. My body just doesn't respond to calcium."
- Observes: years of pills, no DEXA improvement, still shrinking or still fracturing.
- Permission to delay: "I take my calcium every day, so I'm covered." + "Supplements are useless anyway, Tim Spector says so."
- Seriousness: high (diagnosed or fracture-scared), but resigned.

**About solutions**
- Thinks will work: more calcium, the prescribed pills, bisphosphonates, HRT, diet.

- Why she thinks they failed: "My body doesn't absorb it" / "I'm just unlucky" / "it's genetic."

**About herself**
- Self-blame: some, but more anger at the GP/system ("lost faith in all gp services").
- Powerless: "wonder if they even make a difference", "a complete waste of time."
- Identity: the sceptic who has been let down and is done being fobbed off.

**About timing**
- Why not acted on a new route: believes she is already covered by the pill.
- Later feels acceptable: "the GP is monitoring me."

## STEP 3: The 7 angles

### ANGLE 1: "Your calcium pill was never a treatment. It was only ever a top-up, and you leaned the whole house on it."
- **Tier:** 2 (why solutions failed)
- **Current belief:** "I take my calcium every day, so I'm covered."
- **New information:** the bone authorities themselves classify a calcium tablet as an *adjunct*, a top-up that supports the real bone-protective levers, never a standalone treatment. You were handed a supporting player and told it was the answer.
- **Reframe:** it is not that your body failed the calcium. You were given a sidekick and sold it as the hero.
- **Natural conclusion:** I need the actual material bone is built from, food-form calcium, not a top-up tablet I built my whole plan on.
- **Proof:** the NHS / patient-leaflet framing of calcium+D as "support", not treatment.
- **Evidence: Strong** (WHC "adjunct not treatment", verified). **Forehead-slap: high.**

### ANGLE 2: "The chalky feeling and the constipation weren't side effects. They were the calcium failing to get in."
- **Tier:** 1 (reframe what she already felt)
- **Current belief:** "My body just doesn't respond. It's genetic that it didn't work for me."
- **New information:** the body can only take in a small amount of calcium at once. A pill delivers far more in one go, so most of it sits in the gut (the bound-up, "concrete" feeling) and passes straight out. The discomfort you felt was the calcium not being absorbed.
- **Reframe:** you were never a non-responder. The delivery failed, and your gut was telling you so the whole time.
- **Natural conclusion:** I need calcium delivered the way the body actually absorbs it, in small steady amounts in food, not a slug it rejects.
- **Proof:** she can recall the gut discomfort herself; the per-dose absorption cap is documented.
- **Evidence: Strong** (absorption cap + GI intolerance, verified). **Verification: immediate.**

### ANGLE 3: "The inch you blamed on your posture is the first thing bone loss does on the outside."
- **Tier:** 1 (reframe what she observes)
- **Current belief:** "I am getting a bit shorter. It is my age, or my posture."
- **New information:** height loss from the spine is small vertebrae compressing, the first *visible* sign of bone thinning, not posture, not shoes.
- **Reframe:** the shrinking is not cosmetic ageing. It is the inside reporting out.
- **Natural conclusion:** this is bone, it is happening now, and now is the window to feed it.
- **Proof:** the 60-second doorframe / passport-height check.
- **Evidence: Strong** (ROS/NHS: height loss = vertebral fracture). **Verification: tonight, in her own hall.**

### ANGLE 4: "It was never a calcium shortage. After menopause your bones lose faster than any diet replaces, and calcium alone can't switch the building back on."
- **Tier:** 3 (hidden mechanism)
- **Current belief:** "If I just get enough calcium, I will be fine."
- **New information:** the menopausal drop in oestrogen speeds up bone breakdown. Calcium is only the raw material; it does not trigger building, and it has to arrive with its natural team (the things it comes with in food) to be laid into bone. A lone mineral is bricks with no builders.
- **Reframe:** you do not have a calcium-quantity problem. You have a delivery-and-team problem, driven by the change.
- **Natural conclusion:** I need calcium arriving with its team, the way food delivers it, not more of one lone mineral.
- **Proof:** the menopause-bone link (ROS, Women's Health Concern).
- **Evidence: Strong** on the oestrogen mechanism; **Moderate** on the exact co-factor story (keep the "team/builders" framing simple, do not over-claim specific biochemistry).

### ANGLE 5: "Your bones can lose a large share of their strength before a single scan or symptom ever warns you."
- **Tier:** 4 (unknown problem)
- **Current belief:** "If something were wrong, my GP or a scan would catch it."
- **New information:** bone thins silently for years with no pain, and most of the fracture risk builds invisibly in the years right after menopause. A scan only flags osteoporosis once a large amount is already gone, which is why most women are diagnosed *after* a break, not before.
- **Reframe:** "no symptoms and a normal scan so far" is not safety. It is the trap, because this is the kind of damage you cannot feel.
- **Natural conclusion:** I cannot wait for a scan to act. The window is now.
- **Proof:** ROS "silent disease" + the reality that diagnosis usually follows the first fracture (Fracture Liaison Service pathway).
- **Evidence: Strong** on silent-disease + post-menopause acceleration. **Flag:** keep the loss "a large share", do NOT state a hard "X% before a scan shows it" number unless sourced (that exact stat in the existing ads is unverified).

### ANGLE 6: "The famous professors are right that the tablets do nothing, and that is exactly why food works."
- **Tier:** 2 (why solutions failed) + reframes the resignation
- **Current belief:** "Supplements are useless anyway, the experts say so, so why bother."
- **New information:** the experts attacking calcium are attacking the *artificial tablets*. The same experts, and your own bone charity, then point you to *food* as the answer. Their argument was never "give up", it was "stop relying on the pill and get it from food."
- **Reframe:** the anti-supplement noise that made you quit is actually pointing straight at the solution. Food, not pills.
- **Natural conclusion:** the move is not another supplement. It is real food calcium.
- **Proof:** the professors' own quotes (they target tablets) + ROS/NHS/BDA "get calcium from food first."
- **Evidence: Strong** (Spector/Cooper attack tablets; food-first is UK consensus). **Judo-flips the strongest objection.**

### ANGLE 7: "The pills didn't fail because of the calcium. They failed because nobody can take chalk every day for years."
- **Tier:** 2 (why solutions failed, adherence)
- **Current belief:** "I tried the pills, they did not work, so calcium does not work for me."
- **New information:** bone responds to what you do every day for *years*, not for a few months. The carbonate pills that bind you up or turn your stomach are exactly the ones people quietly abandon. The thing that "did not work" is the thing you could never keep up.
- **Reframe:** it was not a failure of calcium. It was a failure of a delivery you could never sustain.
- **Natural conclusion:** I need a form gentle and pleasant enough that I will actually do it every morning for years.
- **Proof:** her own history of stopping the pills; the gut complaints across the category.
- **Evidence: Strong** (GI-driven abandonment is all over the corpus; adherence drives bone outcomes).
