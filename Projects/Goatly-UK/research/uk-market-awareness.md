## B. Total Addressable Market (TAM)

**Addressable population (real, cited):**
- **21.8 to 21.9% of UK women aged 50+ have osteoporosis** (vs 6.7 to 6.8% of men). [IOF SCOPE 2021](https://www.osteoporosis.foundation/sites/iofbonehealth/files/scope-2021/UK%20report.pdf), [NOGG 2024](https://www.nogg.org.uk/full-guideline/section-2-introduction-osteoporosis-and-fragility-fractures).
- **~3.5 million Britons have osteoporosis, ~78% women (~2.95m women).** [Royal Osteoporosis Society](https://theros.org.uk/about-us/media-centre/media-toolkit/).
- **1 in 2 UK women over 50 will break a bone** because of osteoporosis (vs 1 in 5 men). UK-specific; the global figure is 1 in 3. [ROS](https://theros.org.uk/latest-news/women-s-health-strategy-needs-to-go-big-on-osteoporosis-emergency-affecting-one-in-two-women-over-50/).
- Recorded diagnosis incidence ~5x higher in women than men ([Avgerinou 2023, Osteoporosis Int](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10382342/)), and acknowledged to under-count true burden.

**Market value (real, cited, with the honest caveat that a clean UK-only calcium figure does not exist):**
- **Global bone-health supplements: USD 13.8bn (2024) to USD 23.1bn (2034), 5.3% CAGR.** [Allied Market Research](https://www.alliedmarketresearch.com/bone-health-supplements-market-A294439). Caveat: cross-firm 2024 estimates vary ~4x (Precedence USD 4.4bn, Cognitive USD 12.5bn, GVR USD 14.2bn bone+joint, FMI USD 19.5bn) due to scope. Treat as directional.
- **UK menopause market (≈98% supplements): USD 490.1m (2024) to USD 648.1m (2030), 4.8% CAGR.** [Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/horizon/outlook/menopause-market/uk). Closest UK-specific proxy.
- ⚠️ **Do NOT use** the Credence Research "UK calcium supplement market USD 283.71m" figure: it was refuted 0-3 in verification.

**Bottom line:** the addressable population is large and well-quantified (millions of UK women 50+, half of whom will fracture). The precise UK market value is not cleanly available without a paid Mintel/Euromonitor/Statista report.

## C. Awareness stage breakdown (triangulated estimate)

> Two lenses, because they differ and both matter.

### Lens 1: the broad UK women-50+ population (the full TAM)
Osteoporosis is "the silent disease." Most women do not know their bones are weakening until a DEXA scan or a fracture (the NHS Fracture Liaison Service pathway is the dominant trigger, per [[uk-macro-avatars]]). So the broad population skews **Unaware / Problem-Aware about their own bone status.**

### Lens 2: the in-market, ad-responsive audience (who your ads actually reach)
People who engage with bone/menopause content and would respond to a supplement ad skew far more aware. This is the lens that matters for media buying.

**Estimated distribution of the in-market UK bone-health audience** (ordering = HIGH confidence; exact % = directional estimate):

| Stage | % (est.) | Confidence | Evidence base |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware | 10 to 15% | Med | Silent-disease cohort; some in-market still underrate severity ("very worried, don't want to google") |
| Problem-Aware | 20 to 25% | Med-High | Know bones weaken at menopause but haven't acted; "never told the oestrogen-bone link" gap |
| Solution-Aware | **35 to 40%** | High | Know calcium / vitamin D / exercise / diet are the answers, actively researching. The dominant stage. |
| Product-Aware (competitor brands) | 25 to 30% | High | Fluent in AdCal, Calcichew, Osteocare, bisphosphonates, HRT; comparing them |
| Most-Aware (competitor brands) | 5 to 10% | Med | Post-diagnosis veterans comparing specific treatments and brands by T-score response |

**Dominant: Solution-Aware, shading into Product-Aware.**

**Representative language by stage (verbatim, from the corpus):**
- *Problem-Aware:* "Much to my shock, it showed I have osteoporosis"; "I'm very worried about the whole thing."
- *Solution-Aware:* "Make sure you have a high calcium diet, that is a must"; "I started Vit D3, K2 Mg and doing a lot more bouncing around."
- *Product-Aware:* "Calcichew is carbonate which gives my constipation"; "I take calcium citrate because they're gentle on my stomach"; "I don't want to take bisphonates."
- *Most-Aware:* "my Dexa Reading has worsened after three years of one caplet of Adcal d3, a k7 tablet and an extra d3 supplement"; "AdCal D3 is a complete waste of time."

### Lens 3 (the crux): awareness of GOATLY's mechanism
For the specific idea that **goat milk / food-form calcium is a bone solution, the UK market is ~95%+ UNAWARE.** No UK brand positions goat milk for bones (only St Helen's Farm's single absorption line); the academic link is one weak 2024 review citation, and the supporting science is thin ([the strongest study, Kruger 2008, found increased calcium absorption did not increase femur/spine mineral retention](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11263276/)). Goatly's mechanism is greenfield. (Science-strength is a compliance call for [[me|Talat]] per vault rule 16; flagged so the claim can be sourced.)

## E. Failed solutions analysis (quantified from the real corpus)

This section IS well-evidenced. Ranked by how often each appeared as a tried-and-disappointing solution across HealthUnlocked + Mumsnet + Amazon.

1. **Calcium-carbonate pills (AdCal-D3, Calcichew, Osteocare)**, the most-mentioned failed solution.
   - Why tried: GP-prescribed or the default OTC bone product.
   - Why it failed: no improvement on DEXA ("complete waste of time, took it 20 years, still osteoporosis"); chalky/can't swallow ("HUGE tablets, impossible to swallow"); GI ("concrete in my gut", constipation, "diarrhoea... the leaflet claimed no side effects").
2. **Bisphosphonates (alendronic acid, risedronate, zoledronic, Prolia)**, high refusal/fear.
   - Why tried: first-line GP offer after diagnosis.
   - Why it failed/rejected: "I don't want to take bisphonates"; feared side effects; "I felt I was pushed down this route, do I regret it, yes."
3. **HRT**, protective but fear-blocked.
   - Why tried: menopause-bone link, Newson influence.
   - Why mixed: cancer fear ("my mother died from breast cancer after HRT so I don't take it"); some embrace it.
4. **Dietary calcium alone (milk, dairy, greens, tinned fish)**, the ROS/NHS-endorsed first line.
   - Why tried: authorities say food-first.
   - Why it falls short: "I try drinking 1 pint of milk a day plus yoghurt and green veg but it is very hard."
5. **Weight-bearing / resistance exercise**, endorsed, hard to sustain.
   - Why it falls short: fear of fracturing during it; "the vast majority cannot commit to enough exercise."
6. **Vitamin D + K2 (self-directed stack)**: the forum-savvy add-on, uncertain payoff.
7. **Giving up / "natural route"**: after pills disappoint: "I hate taking drugs, shall try natural route." ← Goatly's exact opening.

> **Strategic read:** every dominant failed solution is a PILL or a DRUG. The disappointment with pills is the wedge, and "natural route after pills failed" is the segment actively reaching for what Goatly is.

## F. Final awareness determination

**Category (calcium/bone supplements): the UK market is SOLUTION-AWARE, shading into PRODUCT-AWARE.**
- Buyers know the problem (bones weaken at menopause) and the solution class (calcium, D, exercise, diet, drugs).
- They are fluent in competitor products (AdCal, Calcichew, Osteocare, bisphosphonates, HRT) and compare them.
- Incumbents sell at this level: Osteocare leads with "The UK's No.1 Calcium Supplement Brand" (assumes the problem, offers the trusted answer); AdCal-D3 serves the already-diagnosed.

**Goatly's mechanism (goat milk for bones): UNAWARE.** Nobody in the UK makes the link.

**Cross-source agreement:** corpus (solution/product-aware) + competitor framing (solution-aware) + market data (a mature, served category) all agree. Confidence: **High on the determination, Medium on exact percentages.**

### The Schwartz play this implies
This is the textbook case for **leading with a NEW MECHANISM.** When a market is solution-aware and **tired of / disappointed by the existing solution** (and the UK corpus is full of "the pills did nothing"), you do not educate the problem and you do not out-claim the incumbent. You introduce a **new mechanism for the problem they already understand**: *it was never the pills, it is real food-form calcium your body actually uses.* That reframes Goatly against a Solution/Product-Aware audience while exploiting the disappointment, and it sidesteps the "wrong form of calcium" trap (which ROS undercuts) by pivoting to **food vs pill** and **gut tolerability**. See the wedge analysis in [[uk-voc-absorption-and-goatmilk]] §6.

## H. Executive summary

- **Market awareness level:** **Solution-Aware → Product-Aware** for the calcium/bone category (est. ~60 to 70% of the in-market audience sits at Solution-or-Product-Aware). **Unaware** for Goatly's goat-milk mechanism (~95%+).
- **What they know vs don't:** they know bones weaken with menopause and that calcium/D/exercise/drugs are the answers, and they know the incumbent pills by name. They do NOT know goat-milk/food-form calcium is a bone option, and many are quietly disappointed the pills they take aren't working.
- **Confidence:** High on the determination and ordering; Medium on exact percentages (estimated, not mined); the search-volume sub-section is an open gap needing a paid keyword pull.
- **Most common failed solutions (for mechanism work):** 1) calcium-carbonate pills (AdCal/Calcichew/Osteocare), 2) bisphosphonates, 3) HRT, 4) diet alone. All pills/drugs, which is precisely Goatly's opening.
- **The move:** lead with the new mechanism (food not pills), against a solution-aware, pill-disappointed audience.
